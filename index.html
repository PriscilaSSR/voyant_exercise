The labor struggle in context
"Business partner," protested Grace. "Of course, of course," he replied.
"I'm a damn good perfumer," said Grace. In this, at least, she…
"I can't leave my shop," said Grace. "This is what I do…"

Pattern: Grace's labor is acknowledged in formal announcements ("master perfumer") but minimized in conversational register — she is called "assistant" rather than "business partner." Her insistence on claiming her own title performs exactly what D'Ignazio & Klein call making labor visible. Her expertise drives the plot, yet the data shows it is constantly undervalued by other characters.

The colonial gap is stark. Paladin's Grace: 8 colonial terms total. Manuscript: 33. The fantasy world of Paladin's Grace displaces racial and colonial conflict into the past or into city-as-backdrop, effectively depoliticizing geography. The absence of colonial tension is a choice, not a neutral condition — the "view from nowhere" that D'Ignazio & Klein's third principle asks us to make visible.

The voice suppression identified in the feminist reading is not merely gendered — it is colonial. Vivika whispers and stands in silence because she inhabits two systems of subordination simultaneously: as a woman and as a colonized or semi-colonized subject. This is what Crenshaw's intersectionality, cited by D'Ignazio & Klein, makes visible: her marginalization cannot be reduced to either axis alone.

The manuscript is doing something more politically ambitious than Paladin's Grace — it builds a colonial world and places its protagonists in genuine structural tension with it. Cassius names imperial extraction as extortion; Vivika's silence is produced by a colonial legal order, not just by gender. The feminist reading becomes richer once we see that Vivika's suppressed voice is the product of two systems operating simultaneously. Paladin's Grace sidesteps this question — making it a more comfortable read, but a less politically interrogative one. The manuscript's challenge is to give Vivika's linguistic knowledge the same epistemological authority that Grace's olfactory expertise already carries.
