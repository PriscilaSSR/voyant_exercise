Two characters, two data portraits

The structural finding: The subject/object asymmetry (38.7% vs 27.4%) and the affect gap (60 vs 36 tokens) together suggest a classical masculine/feminine construction — he does, she feels. But the feminist reading complicates this. Vivika's data shows a rich interior life that is actively suppressed. Cassius's shows sparse but significant breaks in a performed composure. Neither is simply what the surface reading suggests.

The affect gap in detail

Vivika's affect vocabulary is both richer and more suppressed. The words cluster around bodily restraint — whispered, swallowed, gulped, biting her lip. Cassius's affect words are sparser but include rare, structurally significant exposures.

The confession that didn't happen: The word confessed appears exactly once in Cassius's 437 contexts — as a conditional clause: "What would Sabine think if Cassius confessed that's how he saw her?" He imagines disclosure and withholds it. The data structure enacts the emotional structure.
